The Crisis

The biggest threat that Pakistan is facing is not its current political tribulations or its endemic terrorism; its the threat of losing a basic human necessity. The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has recently ranked Pakistan third among countries across the world that are facing severe water shortages. The unfortunate reality is that the population in Pakistan continues to rapidly grow, while the supply of fresh water is continuing to decline as fast. The Pakistan Council of Research in Water Resources warned the public that the country would run out of drinkable water by 2025. The country crossed the "water scarcity line" in 2005, resulting in worsening drought-like conditions for the Pakistani people.

In light of this unfortunate reality, Paani is an organization based in Ann Arbor, Michigan, USA that strives to create both short-term and long-term solutions to create sustainable change within the water crisis. Our current partnerships have allowed us to build wells in Pakistan with the help of non-profits like Baitulmaal and Hassan Foundation. We are currently working with engineers to continue to improve the design of these wells to increase the efficiency of water retrieval and increase its resistance to weather/natural disasters.

The versatility of water also affects life-changing institutions such as Mehran Charitable Hospital. Organizations like this provide free health check-ups for the vulnerable and poor who are not able to afford treatments in larger hospitals due to financial or distance barriers. We have had the opportunity to work with this health clinic to increase its efficiency and facilitate its switch to renewable energy to decrease costs and increase the number of patients that can be seen.

Recognizing our privilege in being raised in an area that is surrounded by 25% of the world's fresh water, we have been inspired to work on creating systemic change within Pakistan. Supporting the implementation of water systems in Pakistan has never been a political issue; it was and continues to be a humanitarian issue.

- Paani Executive Team
